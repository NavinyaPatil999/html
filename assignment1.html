The Majestic Tiger: A Detailed Overview

Dive into the world of one of Earth's most powerful and iconic predators. This document provides a comprehensive collection of facts about tigers.
Prepare to be impressed by these incredible felines!

Here's a majestic tiger for your viewing pleasure:


Image Source: Wikimedia Commons

General Characteristics (Felidae Family)

Tigers are the largest of all wild cats and belong to the genus Panthera.

  • Physical Traits:

    Tigers are recognized by their unique pattern of dark vertical stripes on reddish-orange fur with a lighter underside.
    Each tiger's stripe pattern is as unique as a human's fingerprint.

  • Size:

    Male tigers can weigh up to 300 kg (660 lbs) and measure over 3 meters (10 feet) in length, including the tail.

    A tiger's average tail length is approximately 1 meter (3.3 feet).

Habitat & Conservation (Threatened Species)

Tigers primarily inhabit Asian forests, grasslands, and swamps. They are an endangered species.

  1. Geographic Range: Locations include: India, Nepal, Bhutan, Bangladesh, Myanmar, China, Russia, and parts of Southeast Asia.

    Historically, tigers ranged much further, but habitat loss and poaching have severely reduced their numbers.

  2. Conservation Status: IUCN Red List: Endangered

    Subspecies like the Siberian Tiger (Panthera tigris altaica) and the Sumatran Tiger (Panthera tigris sumatrae) are critically endangered.

Tiger Subspecies Overview

There are currently six recognized tiger subspecies still existing in the wild:

Currently Recognized Tiger Subspecies
Subspecies Name Primary Region Status (approx.) Notable Fact
Bengal Tiger India, Bangladesh Endangered Most numerous subspecies.
Indochinese Tiger Southeast Asia Endangered Smaller than Bengal tigers.
Malayan Tiger Malay Peninsula Critically Endangered Genetically distinct from Indochinese.
Siberian Tiger Russian Far East Endangered Largest living feline.
South China Tiger China (possibly extinct in wild) Critically Endangered Ancestral tiger subspecies.
Sumatran Tiger Sumatra Island, Indonesia Critically Endangered Smallest living tiger subspecies.
Three subspecies (Caspian, Javan, Bali) are already extinct.

Behavior & Diet (Apex Predators)

Tigers are solitary hunters and highly territorial animals.

  • Hunting Habits:

    They are ambush predators, often hunting at night. Their diet primarily consists of deer, wild boar, and sometimes buffalo.

    A tiger's roar can be heard up to 3 km (1.86 miles) away!

  • Swimming:

    Unlike most cats, tigers are excellent swimmers and often use water to cool down or cross rivers in pursuit of prey.
